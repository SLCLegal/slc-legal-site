Common matters

Tell us which problem sounds most like yours.

Most people contacting a lawyer are not trying to classify their case perfectly. They are trying to figure out whether what happened to them is serious, unlawful, and worth pursuing. Start with the issue that feels closest.

Retaliation and wrongful termination You complained, requested leave, asked for accommodation, reported unlawful conduct, raised pay issues, or protected yourself at work, and the employer turned against you.
Discrimination and harassment Pregnancy, disability, medical condition, race, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, or age affected how you were treated.
Wage and hour Unpaid overtime, salary misclassification, missed breaks, late final pay, inaccurate wage statements, or broader pay practices affecting groups of workers.
Leave and accommodation You requested medical leave, work restrictions, schedule changes, or accommodation, and the employer ignored, delayed, denied, or punished you.
Whistleblower and Labor Code retaliation You reported safety concerns, unlawful conduct, fraud, patient-care issues, or wage violations and were disciplined, pushed out, or terminated.
Personal injury Auto collisions, premises liability incidents, and other injury cases where liability, treatment, documentation, and preservation need to be handled seriously from the beginning.
