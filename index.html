Common situations

You do not need to know the legal label before reaching out.

What matters at the beginning is what happened, when it happened, who was involved, what changed afterward, and what records still exist.

Retaliation and wrongful termination You complained, reported a problem, requested leave, asked for accommodation, or asserted your rights, and your employer turned against you.
Discrimination and harassment Pregnancy, disability, medical condition, race, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, or age changed how you were treated.
Wage and hour Unpaid overtime, salary misclassification, missed breaks, late final pay, wage statement issues, or broader pay practices affecting more than one worker.
Leave and accommodation You needed time off, restrictions, or changes at work, and the response was delay, denial, punishment, or pressure.
Whistleblower and Labor Code retaliation You reported unlawful conduct, safety concerns, fraud, patient-care issues, or wage violations and were disciplined, pushed out, or terminated.
Personal injury You were hurt in a collision, a fall, or another serious incident and need the treatment, proof, and damage picture handled carefully from the beginning.

If it feels like everything changed at once, start with what you still have.

A workplace problem can quickly become a pay problem, a health problem, a family problem, or a question of how much stability was lost. A serious injury can become a treatment problem, an income problem, and a long disruption to daily life.

You do not need to sort out every legal issue before reaching out. Start with the facts you still have.

  • Messages or emails
  • Dates and timeline
  • Pay records
  • Write-ups or notices
  • Names of witnesses
  • Photos
  • Treatment records
  • Anything showing what changed

Begin while the record can still be put together carefully.

The first step is not to phrase everything perfectly. The first step is to begin while the details are still clear enough to preserve.
